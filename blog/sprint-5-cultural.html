Sprint 5 Cultural

What are three new things you have learned about yourself and your ego as a result of the core learning?
  • Try not to over-think a seemingly minor problem where possible.
  • Everyone is here to help out each other when encountering difficulty, and to understand one another's traits.
  • Piece by piece often does the trick.
What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

The importance of being in the right frame of mind.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Sticking to the recommended scheules was a challenge at times, for someone who's accustomed to making things up as they go along. Also, putting thoughts to (digital) paper as a naturally self-effacing type took some getting used to.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

Because soft skills are a major part of being a web developer and the wider ICT sector.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practicing programming instead? Justify your answer.

Not necessarily. It's one thing to be a coding expert, but it's quite another to make it as one in isolation. Technical skills alone don't cut it as much as they used to, as a rapidly-changing ICT industry increasingly requires wider skill sets.
